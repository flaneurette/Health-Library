# Styrofoam repurposing.

Sometimes we get a pacakge that still contains styrofoam. I don't like tossing it away as it's bad for the environment. Here are a few hacks we can use to repurpose styrofoam endlessly.

> TIP: Safety concern: styrofoam is flammable, so keep away from electricity and flammable items.

---

## 10+ Styrofoam Hacks / Reuse Ideas

### 1. Plant & Gardening Hacks

* Pot drainage: Break into small chunks-> bottom of plant pots-> better drainage.
* Soil aeration: Mix into potting soil-> keeps soil light.
* Seedling trays: Use clean Styrofoam trays-> start seeds without buying special trays.
* Frost protection: Wrap plants during cold snaps-> acts as insulation.

---

### 2. Packaging & Storage

* Protect fragile items: Wrap glassware, electronics, or bottles when storing or shipping.
* Reusable padding: Keep broken chunks to use in future boxes-> saves on bubble wrap.
* Custom inserts: Cut to size-> hold delicate items in drawers or boxes.

---

### 3. Home / Energy Hacks

* Window draft stopper: Cut into strips-> block gaps under doors/windows.
* Cooler lining: Add inside coolers or lunch boxes-> keeps things cold longer.
* Floor insulation: Place under plant pots or fragile floor items to prevent scratches and insulate.

---

### 4. Art & DIY

* Mini sculptures / models: Lightweight, easy to carve or cut.
* Stamping / printing: Carve shapes-> dip in paint-> stamp on paper or fabric.
* Holiday decorations: Spray paint sheets-> make snow, stars, or wall décor.

---

### 5. Furniture / Practical Builds

* Lightweight shelves / storage boxes: Large Styrofoam blocks + tape/plaster-> temporary shelving.
* Cushioning for seating: Layer thin sheets in chair seats-> adds padding.

---

### 6. Miscellaneous Life Hacks

* Craft organizer: Use small chunks to prop or separate tools, jewelry, or utensils in drawers.
* Pet bed filler: Clean Styrofoam peanuts-> use inside pet bed for lightweight cushion.
* Shipping eco-hack: Keep broken chunks-> reuse instead of buying bubble wrap or peanuts.

---

Supersaver mindset:

* Break, cut, or flatten everything first-> easier to store and reuse.
* Combine multiple uses: For example, broken chunks-> plant drainage + drawer organizer + shipping padding.
* Styrofoam never rots, so once it’s in your system, it keeps giving utility.
